And then, there was Isaac Asimov

After stumbling upon "Robot Dreams" by Isaac Asimov, Bobby knew his life had changed forever. It was no longer feasible to think of humans as the only beings with the internal struggles of doing the right thing—there was now a much greater ideal to strive toward—the ideal of a better world, where robots and humans lived in intelligent harmony.

Bobby set out on a mission to model and recreate the brilliance of the robots famed in Asimov's stories. Little did he realize that this would set him on the path toward some of his most surprising creations.

Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics:

Rule #1

A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.

Rule #2

A robot must obey orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.

Rule #3

A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.
